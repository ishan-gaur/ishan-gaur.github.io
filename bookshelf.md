## Bookshelf

The books I own or remember reading at some point, in no particular order. If I haven't read it yet, the subtitle and author are not included. For the ones where I recall, I've also included who I heard about the book from.

- The Dappled World: A Study of the Boundaries of Science (Natalie Cartwright)
- The Divide: Global Inequality from Conquest to Free Markets (Jason Hickel, recommended by Jay Mathuri)
- Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind (Yuval Noah Harari)
- Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity (Peter Attia)
- Pioneering Portfolio Management: An Unconventional Approach to Institutional Investment (David F. Swensen)
- A More Beautiful and Terrible History: The Uses and Misuses of Civil Rights History (Jeanne Theoharis, recommended by [Jim Campbell](https://history.stanford.edu/people/james-campbell))
- Man's Search for Meaning (Victor Frankl)
- What is Life? (Erwin Schrödinger)
- The Structure of Scientific Revolutions (Thomas S. Kuhn)
- In Struggle: SNCC and the Black Awakening of the 1960s (Clayborne Carson, recommended by [Jim Campbell](https://history.stanford.edu/people/james-campbell))
- Kochland: The Secret Hisotry of Koch Industries and Corporate Power in America (Christopher Leonard, recommended by [Vincent Song](https://www.vincentjsong.com/))
- Vagabonding (Rolf Potts, recommended by Tim Ferris)
- The Art of Doing Science and Engineering: Learning to Learn (Richard W. Hamming, recommended by [Patrick Collison](https://patrickcollison.com/))
- The Rise and Fall of Modern Medicine (recommended by [Patrick Collison](https://patrickcollison.com/))
- The Autobiography of Benjamin Franklin (Big Ben, recommended by [Charlie Munger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charlie_Munger))
- Edison (recommended by [David Senra](https://www.davidsenra.com/))
- Endless Frontier (recommended by [David Senra](https://www.davidsenra.com/))
- The Intel Trinity (recommended by [David Senra](https://www.davidsenra.com/))
- How to Win Freinds and Influence People
- A Short Hostory of Chinese Philosophy: A Systematic Account of Chinese Thought from Its Origins to the Present Day (Fung Yu-Lan)
- The Profiteers
- The Count of Monte Cristo (recommended by [Chris Fizer](https://www.instagram.com/chrisfizer_/))
- A Critical Survey of Indian Philosophy (Chandradhar Sharma, recommended by [Vikas Divyakirti](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vCEDptvB5Q4))
- Sophie's World (recommended by [Vikas Divyakirti](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vCEDptvB5Q4))
- The Age of Insight (Eric R. Kandel, recommended by [Wei Ouyang](https://aicell.io/authors/wei/))
